# QL21 — Survey Insights
## The 21st Century Lab Notebook — Research Findings

> Thank you for participating in this survey. Here are the key findings from the 113 responses collected across UAB Campus research groups in May 2026.

---

## Who responded?

113 researchers, students, and technical staff across 11 departments at UAB Campus, covering disciplines from nanoscience and microelectronics to biochemistry, computer science, and telecommunications.

| Role | Responses |
|------|-----------|
| Senior Researcher / Staff Scientist | 33 |
| Research Group Leader | 27 |
| PhD Student | 24 |
| Postdoctoral Researcher | 13 |
| Engineer / Technical Staff | 10 |
| Project Manager | 5 |
| Undergraduate / Master Student | 1 |

---

## Key findings

### Reproducibility is genuinely difficult

> **Mean difficulty score: 3.08 / 5**

38 out of 113 respondents rated reproducing past experiments as Hard or Impossible based on existing notes. Only 2 rated it as Very Easy.

### Current tools are not working

The two most commonly used documentation tools are paper notebooks and Microsoft Word, each reported by 44 respondents (38.94%). Together, these tools account for nearly 80% of all responses, indicating a strong reliance on traditional, non-specialized documentation methods. In contrast, structured or version-controlled documentation tools are almost entirely absent, suggesting limited adoption of more formalized documentation practices.

### The biggest documentation nightmares

| Problem | Responses |
|---------|-----------|
| Naming hell (final_v2_NEW_fixed.csv) | 76 |
| Data on inaccessible laptops | 72 |
| Missing metadata | 68 |
| Unreadable handwriting | 50 |
| Lack of time after lab sessions | 33 |

### Most researchers document in real-time

65 out of 113 respondents document in real-time inside the lab. But 42 document after the fact, by which point contextual details are often already lost.

### Interest in structured documentation tools is high

> **68.4% said yes to structured templates**

80 out of 113 respondents said they would use a standardised template if it saves them time. Only 7 said no.

### Most requested features

| Feature | Responses |
|---------|-----------|
| Searchability across all notes | 74 |
| Easy integration of images and plots | 65 |
| Automatic metadata capture | 60 |
| Version control | 47 |

---

## What this means

Several trends emerged consistently across the survey responses:

1. Documentation challenges appear across almost all research groups.
2. current tools do not adequately support reproducibility.
3. Many researchers expressed interest in adopting more structured approaches, provided that the additional workload remains limited.

These findings informed the design priorities of the QL21 framework and its associated documentation templates.
